Special Projects

Developing innovative solutions to emerging student needs and campus challenges

Sunhee Frecker

Director, Special Projects

Sunhee Frecker is a junior majoring in PWAD and Economics. She enjoys her role as Director of Special Projects because she loves having the opportunity to be at the forefront of many of the administration's exciting initiatives. She enjoys having the opportunity to work on different projects and using her creativity. She travelled to Malaysia with the PWAD program this past summer.

Our Purpose

Our Mission

The Special Projects office develops and implements innovative initiatives that respond to emerging student needs and campus challenges. We work across departments and task forces to bring creative solutions to complex problems affecting the student experience.

Our Work

Key Priorities

Emerging Needs

Identifying and responding to emerging student needs through rapid project development and implementation.

Pilot Programs

Developing pilot programs to test innovative solutions before broader implementation across campus.

Cross-Department Work

Coordinating cross-departmental initiatives that require collaboration across multiple student government areas.

Strategic Partnerships

Building partnerships with campus and community stakeholders to expand our impact and resources.

Featured Work

Current Projects

Special Projects currently manages several high-priority initiatives including campus accessibility improvements, student mental health support programs, and sustainability initiatives. Each project is designed to create lasting positive impact on the student experience.
