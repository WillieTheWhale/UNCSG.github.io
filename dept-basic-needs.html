Our Mission

About the Department

The Department of Basic Needs is dedicated to ensuring every student at Carolina has the essential resources to thrive academically, professionally, and personally. Any hardship impacting a student's ability to focus on education and development will be considered a basic need. This includes access to professional clothing, housing, menstrual products, contaminant-free water, food security and meal-related accommodations, and more.

Students' input is central to the department. Needs are assessed through campus-wide surveys and ongoing feedback to continually assess and address emerging needs.

Our Work

Current Initiatives

Carolina Closet

A rental system for students to access professional attire, such as suits, skirts, and dress pants. This initiative eliminates barriers to students' professional development.

Food Insecurity Coalition

Identifying student organizations working to alleviate hunger, encouraging joint efforts, and filling identified gaps in food access across campus.

Housing Initiatives

Working on access to appliances and other housing support to ensure students have safe and comfortable living conditions.

NAVVY Tool Partnership

Partnering with the Student Basic Needs Coalition to help students determine eligibility for government benefit programs, potentially accessing thousands of dollars for groceries.
